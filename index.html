Photography setup

Poor photos produce misleading measurements. Spend a minute getting this right.

Camera position. Exactly at eye level. Not below — a below-eye angle lengthens the face and exaggerates the lower third, which will throw off vertical-thirds and jaw metrics. Not above, which does the opposite.

Framing. Face centred and occupying at least 60% of the frame height. If the face is too small the landmark detector loses sub-pixel precision on the periocular region.

Lighting. Natural window light directly in front of you. Not overhead (casts eye hollows and ages the face). Not lateral (creates false asymmetry in the shadow pattern).

Expression. Neutral. Teeth together, lips closed, jaw relaxed. Any smile, clench, or asymmetric expression will propagate through the mouth and jaw measurements.

Head position. Perfectly level. Use a horizontal reference in the frame if unsure. The tool applies a rotation correction for small tilts but cannot recover meaningful data above ~9° of yaw.

Remove. Glasses, hair covering the forehead or ears, any filter or beauty mode, any AI enhancement from the phone camera. Many modern phones apply smoothing by default — check settings.

First load. The MediaPipe model file is approximately 4MB and loads once on first use, then caches. On a slow connection expect a brief wait.

What this tool cannot assess. Profile measurements (nasolabial angle, E-line, mentocervical angle, gonial angle) are not included — MediaPipe Face Landmarker is trained on frontal faces and produces unreliable output on profiles. Skin quality (texture, pore size, pigmentation, fine lines) is not assessed — that requires a dedicated dermatological computer vision model, not landmark geometry. True bizygomatic width requires a submentovertex radiograph or 3D scan; this tool's value is a soft-tissue approximation. Skeletal asymmetry requires a posteroanterior cephalogram; the symmetry score here is soft-tissue only. Gonial angle cannot be reliably measured from a frontal photo, so the jaw/cheekbone ratio is a soft-tissue jaw width estimate, not a bony angle.
